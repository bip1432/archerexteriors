Best Siding for Your Home

Durability

New Jersey has a diverse climate, ranging from hot and humid summers to cold and snowy winters. That means your siding needs to withstand temperature fluctuations, moisture, wind, hail, and UV rays. Some of the most durable siding materials are fiber cement, metal, and brick. These materials can last decades without cracking, fading, warping, or rotting. However, they are also more expensive and heavier than other options.

Cost

The cost of siding depends on several factors, such as the type and quality of the material, the size and shape of your house, the amount of labor and preparation required, and the warranty and installation fees. Generally speaking, vinyl siding is the most affordable option, followed by wood and engineered wood. Fiber cement, metal, and brick are more expensive options. However, it would help to consider the long-term maintenance and repair costs when choosing siding.

Maintenance

Different types of siding require different levels of care. Vinyl siding is the easiest to maintain, as it only needs an occasional wash with a hose or a soft brush. Wood siding requires more maintenance, such as painting or staining every few years to prevent fading and decay. Fiber cement siding is relatively low-maintenance but may need repainting every 10 to 15 years. Metal siding is also low-maintenance, but it may dent or scratch easily. Brick siding is virtually maintenance-free but may need tuck-pointing every 20 to 30 years.

Style

The style of your siding should match the architectural style of your home and your personal preference. You can choose from various colors, textures, and patterns to create a unique look for your home. Some of the most popular styles are:

  • Clapboard: Horizontal planks that overlap each other.
  • Shingle: Small pieces that resemble roof shingles.
  • Board-and-batten: Vertical planks with narrow strips covering the seams.
  • Shake: Rough-cut pieces that create a rustic look.
  • Stone or brick veneer: Thin layers that mimic the look of natural stone or brick.

New siding is a very exciting addition to your home. Maintaining your siding is key to ensure its longevity and appearance. Here are some tips on how to maintain your new siding:
  • Regularly clean your siding. Dirt, grime, and debris can accumulate on your siding, leading to discoloration and damage. You can use a power washer or a garden hose to clean your siding. Avoid using high-pressure water to prevent damage to your siding.
  • Inspect your siding for damage. Regular inspections can help you identify any issues early on and prevent them from becoming bigger problems. Check for cracks, dents, or scratches and have the siding repaired immediately.
  • Trim trees and bushes near your siding. Overgrown trees and bushes can scratch and damage your siding. Keep them trimmed to prevent any potential damage.
  • Paint or stain your siding as needed. If you have wood siding, you will need to paint or stain it every few years to protect it from weathering and rot.
By following these tips, you can help maintain your new siding and keep it looking beautiful for years to come. If you have any questions or concerns about your siding maintenance, feel free to contact us at Archer Exteriors.

At Archer Exteriors, we take great care in properly installing vinyl siding to ensure it lasts for years to come. Here's a general overview of our installation process:
  • Preparation: We begin by removing any existing siding and inspecting the exterior of the home for any damage or rot that needs repair. We also install a moisture barrier to prevent water from seeping behind the siding.
  • Mounting: We install J-channel around windows and doors to provide a secure base for the siding. We also install starter strips at the bottom of the wall to hold the first course of siding in place.
  • Siding Installation: We start at the bottom of the wall and work our way up, overlapping each course of siding to prevent gaps and ensure a tight fit. We also make sure to leave room for expansion and contraction due to temperature changes.
  • Finishing Touches: We install trim around windows, doors, and corners to complete the look and provide additional protection from the elements.
