Some of the common signs that indicate your roof needs attention are:
  • Missing, cracked, or curling shingles
  • Leaks or water stains on your ceiling or walls
  • Sagging or buckling of your roof deck
  • Mold or mildew growth on your roof or attic
  • Granules in your gutters or around your house
  • Increased energy bills due to poor insulation
If you notice any of these signs, you should contact us as soon as possible for a free inspection and estimate. We will assess the condition of your roof and recommend the best course of action.

Archer Exteriors offers a wide range of roofing materials to suit your needs, preferences, and budget. Some of the most popular types of roofing materials that we install are:
  • Asphalt shingles: Asphalt are the most common and affordable roofing material in North America. Shingles made of asphalt are easy to install, repair, and replace. Asphalt roofs come in different styles, colors, and designs to match any home style. These shingles have a good fire resistance rating and can withstand moderate winds and hail. However, asphalt shingles have a shorter lifespan than other roofing materials, typically lasting 15 to 20 years. They are also prone to fading, cracking, curling, and algae growth over time. They are not very eco-friendly, as they contribute to landfill waste and greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Metal roofing: These are made from various metals, such as steel, aluminum, copper, or zinc. Metal roofs have a modern and sleek appearance that can complement any home style. Metal roofing materials are extremely durable and long-lasting, with a lifespan of 50 years or more. These roofs are resistant to fire, wind, hail, corrosion, and pests. A good metal roof has a high reflectivity value and can help reduce heat gain and cooling costs. Another bonus to metal roofing is the environmentally friendly factor as they are recyclable and energy-efficient. However, a metal roof can be more expensive than asphalt shingles, both in terms of material and installation costs. They also tend to be noisy during rain or hail storms. Finally, consider that a roof made out of metal may dent or scratch easily if not properly installed or maintained.
  • Tile roofing: Roofs made with tile can be from various materials, such as clay, concrete, or terracotta. Tile roofs have a unique and colorful appearance that can create a Mediterranean or Spanish look for any home style. Tile roofing are very durable and long-lasting, with a lifespan of 50 years or more. These roofs are resistant to fire, wind, hail, water damage, and insects. Tiled roofs have a high insulation value and can help reduce energy costs. They are environmentally friendly, as they are natural and recyclable. However, tile roofing can be very expensive compared to other roofing materials, both in terms of material and installation costs. They also require skilled labor and special tools to install and repair. Tiles are very heavy and may need additional structural support to hold them. Tiled roofing may break or crack if not properly installed or maintained.

The time it takes to replace a roof depends on several factors, such as the type and quantity of the material, the size and complexity of your house, the weather conditions, and the availability of the crew. Generally speaking, it takes about one to three days to replace a roof with asphalt shingles, and about three to five days to replace a roof with metal or tile roofing. However, these times are only estimates and may vary depending on your specific situation. Roof flashing is a thin material, typically made of metal, that is installed on the roof to prevent water from seeping into the roofing system. It is usually placed around areas of the roof where water is most likely to penetrate, such as chimneys, vents, skylights, and valleys.

The purpose of roof flashing is to redirect water away from these vulnerable areas and onto the shingles or tiles, where it can safely drain off the roof. Without proper flashing, water can seep into the roof and cause damage to the underlying structure, as well as lead to leaks and other problems.

At Archer Exteriors, we specialize in installing and repairing all types of roof flashing to ensure that your roof is protected from water damage.

Drip Edge is a type of flashing that is installed at the edges of a roof to prevent water from seeping under the roofing material and into the underlying structure. It is typically made of metal or plastic and is shaped like an L or T to fit over the edge of the roof.

Drip Edge is an important component of any roofing system as it helps to protect the roof and the structure of the building from water damage. At Archer Exteriors, we offer high-quality Drip Edge installation services to ensure your roof is properly protected. Contact us today to learn more.

Underlayment is a layer of material that is installed between the roof deck and the shingles or other roofing material. It serves as a secondary layer of protection against water infiltration and helps to keep the roof deck dry. Underlayment is an essential component of any roofing system and is available in a variety of materials, including felt, synthetic, and rubberized asphalt.

At Archer Exteriors, we only use high-quality underlayment materials to ensure the longevity and durability of your roof. Our team of experienced professionals can help you choose the best underlayment for your roof based on your specific needs and budget. Contact us today to learn more about our roofing services.

The lifespan of a roof can vary depending on the type of material used, the quality of installation, and the climate. Generally speaking, a standard asphalt shingle roof can last anywhere from 15 to 25 years. However, some high-quality shingle roofs can last up to 50 years.

Metal roofs can last even longer, with a lifespan of 50 to 75 years. Other factors that can affect the lifespan of a roof include the maintenance it receives, the frequency of severe weather events, and the level of exposure to sunlight.

It's important to have your roof inspected regularly by a professional to ensure that it remains in good condition and to address any issues before they become major problems.
